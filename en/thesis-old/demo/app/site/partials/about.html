About the Dynamic Emotion Wheel

The site is currently under development and so is the technical documentation of the tool.

For instance, the most comprehensive resource about the Dynamic Emotion Wheel is Mattia A. Fritz's Master Thesis. The thesis comprises:

  • A theoretical framework covering aspects related to emotions, in particular the Component Process Model framework, and awareness in Computer-Supported Collaboration, especially Computer-Supported Collaborative Learning.
  • The description of the most important phases of the development cycle of the first version of the tool, with respect to both human-computer interaction and technical choices.
  • The results of a usability test conducted in experimental settings with 16 participants undergoing a simulated collaborative task.

The manuscript is available through the Open Archive of the Geneva University.

Future developments

The tool needs further development both at theoretical and technical level in order to fulfill its role of emotional awareness and self-report tool.

At a theoretical level, the tool still needs:

  • More and more accurate underlying circumplexes based on the GRID instrument. The position of subjective feelings on the wheel must be empirically determined with a different tool than the DEW itself for consistency and accuracy.
  • More variety in graphical emotion representation (i.e. other tools than the emotion timeline and the appraisal graphics), especially with respect to integrated or grouped representations (e.g. tag clouds).

At a technical level, the tool still needs:

  • A functioning prototype that allows researchers to test their configuration and experimental settings.
  • A version of the tool that can be easily shared with and installed by researchers.
  • A backend interface to check and export results.
