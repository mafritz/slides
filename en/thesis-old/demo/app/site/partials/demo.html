The DEW uses two different algorithms to compute a subset of adjacent emotions according to two-dimensional quantitative values.

Radial distance

In oder to calculate the radial distance, subjective feelings are "artificially" arranged on a circle and associated with an angle from the Y axis. The algorithm computes the angle of a hypothetical radius starting from the center of the circumplex and crossing a point given by the coordinates on dimensions X and Y. The feelings closest to the computed angle are part of the subset.

Vector distance

In order to calculate the vector distance, subjective feelings are arranged on a scatterplot based on their "coordinates" on dimensions X and Y. The algorithm computes the distance from the position of feelings and the coordinates of the appraised situation on the two dimensions, calculating the hypotenuse of the triangle formed by the distances on X and Y. The closest feelings are part of the subset.
